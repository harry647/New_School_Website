School Administration

At Bar Union Mixed Secondary School, our administration champions visionary leadership, effective governance, and a student-first approach to management.

We strive to provide a safe, disciplined, and nurturing environment where both staff and students excel academically, socially, and personally through strategic planning and collaborative teamwork.

Board of Management (BOM)

Providing Visionary Leadership & Strategic Oversight

The Board of Management (BOM) is the highest governing body at Bar Union Mixed Secondary School, entrusted with ensuring strategic direction, policy-making, and holistic management of the school. The BOM upholds the school's core values and guarantees compliance with the Education Act, while also promoting spiritual growth in alignment with the sponsoring African Inland Church.

Overview & Role

  • Oversees school policy, administration, and strategic vision
  • Chairperson (2017–2021): Mr. George Ominde, leading a competent and visionary team
  • Structured into committees handling Finance, Infrastructure, Discipline, and other key areas
  • Principal acts as Secretary, ensuring effective execution of BOM decisions
  • Collaborates closely with Parents' Association (PA) and the School Senior Management Committee (SMC)

Composition

  • Maintains gender balance with at least one-third female members
  • Representative Breakdown:
    • 3 members from the community
    • 4 members from the sponsoring church
    • 3 members from special interest groups
  • Total membership: Approximately 10–12, ensuring diversity, equity, and inclusivity

Supporting Committees

  • Parents' Association (PA)

    Executive arm of BOM, chaired by Mr. Charles Oswago. Includes 3 parent reps per class, 2 teacher reps, Principal & Deputy Principal.

  • School Senior Management Committee (SMC)

    Handles daily school operations, ensuring ministry directives and BOM policies are implemented. Members: Principal, Deputy Principal, Senior Master, HODs.

  • Student Leadership

    Prefects and club leaders link students with the BOM and SMC, ensuring adherence to rules and active participation in school programs.

Organizational Structure

  • BOM sits at the apex, setting vision, policies, and strategic priorities
  • Direct reports include: Principal, Deputy Principal, Director of Studies, and Bursar
  • Downstream structure: HODs, co-curricular coaches, support staff, Prefect Council, and club officials
  • Emphasizes top-down governance with integrated input from teachers, students, and parents

Achievements & Impact

  • Enhanced staff remuneration packages (2015–2019 strategic plan)
  • Improved safety, security, and teaching/learning environment
  • Funds efficiently managed via school fees, government grants, and fundraising initiatives
  • Ensured sustainable school development aligned with strategic priorities

Philosophy & Values

  • Promotes godliness, integrity, professionalism, teamwork, and equity
  • Guarantees equal opportunities for all students, irrespective of tribe, gender, religion, or economic background
  • Supports discipline, guidance, and counselling, with strong parental involvement
  • Maintains alignment with the school's mission to develop morally, spiritually, and academically well-rounded students

Administrative Departments

Essential Teams Driving Academic Excellence, Student Welfare, and Operational Efficiency

At Bar Union Mixed Secondary School, administrative departments form the backbone of our institution. Each department works collaboratively to ensure smooth academic delivery, holistic student development, and effective management of school operations. Together, they create an environment that nurtures learning, leadership, creativity, and well-being.

Academic Department

Oversees curriculum planning, lesson monitoring, teacher professional development, and assessment strategies. Ensures consistent academic standards across all subjects and promotes innovative teaching methods to enhance student learning outcomes.

Finance & Accounts

Manages school finances, including fee collection, budgeting, audits, and reporting. Supports resource allocation for academics, co-curricular activities, infrastructure, and staff welfare while ensuring transparency and accountability.

Administration & Operations

Coordinates daily school operations, facility management, transport, security, and general administration. Ensures a safe, organized, and conducive environment for learning and co-curricular engagement.

Sports & Physical Education

Organizes athletics, team sports, fitness programs, and inter-house competitions. Promotes physical health, teamwork, discipline, and resilience while nurturing talent for county, regional, and national competitions.

Arts & Culture

Encourages creative expression through music, drama, visual arts, and cultural programs. Supports participation in competitions, festivals, and exhibitions to develop creativity, confidence, and appreciation of heritage.

Student Welfare & Guidance

Provides counseling, mentorship, life skills training, and conflict resolution. Monitors student well-being, supports personal growth, and helps students navigate academic, social, and emotional challenges effectively.

Library & Resource Centre

Manages print and digital resources, research facilities, and study spaces. Promotes reading culture, academic research, and independent learning through access to reference materials, journals, and online resources.

ICT & Innovation

Maintains the school's IT infrastructure, digital classrooms, and e-learning platforms. Drives technology integration in teaching, learning, and administration, while fostering innovation and digital literacy among students and staff.
