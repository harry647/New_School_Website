Our Journey Since 1983

Bar Union Mixed Secondary School derives its name from "Bar," a Luo word meaning "a region with plenty of grass and scattered trees." The school was established in 1983 and provisionally registered in 1984 as a union of three churches — Anglican, African Inland, and Seventh Day Adventist — under the guidance of Mr. Daniel Ollo, Mr. Jacob Otiende, and Mr. Ibrahim Ahoya.

From a modest beginning with 50 students and 5 teachers, the school has grown into a four-streamed institution with 615 students and 28 teachers, projected to reach approximately 800 students by 2021.

Over the decades, six principals have guided the school's growth. Currently, Mr. George Achilla serves as Principal & Director, leading the school towards excellence in academics, sports, and leadership.
